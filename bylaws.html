Bylaws of the KSEA Philadelphia Chapter

Approved by the Council on XXX XX, 2025.
Adopted by the General Assembly on XXX XX, 2025.

CHAPTER 1: PURPOSE AND PROGRAMS

ARTICLE 1. Statement of Purpose

Korean-American Scientists and Engineers Association, Philadelphia Chapter ("KSEA Philadelphia") shall be operated exclusively as a non-profit organization exempt from Federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code to achieve the following objectives:

  • Contribute to the social welfare and the industrial development in the Philadelphia area through the promotion of science and engineering.
  • Promote and cultivate the next generation of Korean-American scientists and engineers.
  • Promote fellowship among Korean-American professionals engaged in science, engineering, and related fields.
  • Enhance the professional and social status of Korean-American professionals engaged in science, engineering, and related fields.
  • Promote cooperation between the Philadelphia area and Korea in science and engineering.
  • Represent the body of KSEA members residing in Philadelphia and the adjacent areas.

ARTICLE 2. Programs

  • Activities to advance technical capabilities and industrial development in the Philadelphia area.
  • Programs to promote and cultivate the next generation of Korean-American scientists and engineers.
  • Scholarship and internship programs in the Philadelphia area.
  • Technical conferences, symposiums, and workshops in the Philadelphia area, and the publication and distribution of technical information.
  • Activities to promote friendship and fellowship among the members.
  • Activities to enhance the professional and social status of the members.
  • Activities to promote cooperation between the Philadelphia area and Korea in science and engineering.

CHAPTER 2: MEMBERSHIP

ARTICLE 3. Membership Categories

KSEA Philadelphia membership shall consist of the following four categories:

  • Regular Member
  • Student Member
  • Distinguished Sponsor Member
  • Honorary Member

Admission of members, except Distinguished Sponsors and Honorary Members, is subject to approval of the KSEA Executive Committee.

ARTICLE 4. Qualifications for Regular Members

A regular member shall be a Korean-American professional residing in the Philadelphia area, with at least a bachelor's degree or its equivalent, who is engaged in science, engineering, or a related field.

ARTICLE 5. Qualifications for Student Member

Undergraduate Student Member. An undergraduate student member shall be a college student residing in the Philadelphia area who is pursuing a degree in science, engineering, or a related field. Undergraduate Student members shall not have voting rights.

Graduate Student Member. A graduate student member shall be a graduate student residing in the Philadelphia area who is pursuing a degree in science, engineering, or a related field. Graduate student members shall have the same voting rights as regular members.

ARTICLE 6. Qualifications for Distinguished Sponsor Member and Honorary Member

A KSEA Philadelphia member or committee may submit a nomination for a distinguished sponsor member or honorary member.

Distinguished Sponsor Member. The Executive Committee shall approve a distinguished sponsor member, who is a respectable natural person or legal entity and has made substantial contributions to KSEA Philadelphia. Distinguished sponsor members do not have voting rights.

Honorary Member. The Executive Committee shall approve an honorary member with his or her consent.

ARTICLE 7. Voting Member

A voting member is a regular member or graduate student member who has paid the membership dues at least once within the last two fiscal years (the current and the past).

ARTICLE 8. Admission

Except for members described in Article 6, to become a KSEA Philadelphia member, the prospective member must submit the KSEA membership application form and the annual dues to KSEA or KSEA Philadelphia.

ARTICLE 9. Membership Dues

Membership dues for each membership category shall be determined by KSEA.

ARTICLE 10. Disciplinary Actions

In accordance with the policies and procedures adopted by the Council, the Council may suspend the rights and privileges of membership or expel any member who violates the Bylaws or KSEA Philadelphia policies, interferes with KSEA Philadelphia's goals and business, or disgraces KSEA Philadelphia's reputation.

ARTICLE 11. Meeting of Members ("General Assembly")

Annual Meetings. Members shall meet annually for the purpose of electing councilors and officers and for transacting such other business as may properly come before the meeting.

Special Meetings. A special meeting may be initiated at any time by (i) a majority of the Councilors, or (ii) the President at the written request of at least twenty or one-fifth of the voting members. Such a request shall state the purpose of the meeting.

Notice of Meetings. Written notice of each meeting, stating the time and place, shall be delivered via mail or electronic means not less than twenty (20) days before the meeting to each voting member.

Quorum. The presence of at least twenty (20) voting members or one-fifth of all voting members shall be necessary to constitute a quorum.

Voting. Every voting member shall have one (1) vote. All elections and questions shall be decided by a majority of the votes cast.

CHAPTER 3: BOARD OF DIRECTORS (COUNCIL)

ARTICLE 12. Councilors

A Council shall manage the affairs of the Chapter, consisting of:

  • President
  • Vice President(s)
  • Executive Director
  • Finance Director
  • General Director or Membership Director
  • Immediate Past President(s)
  • Elected Councilors
  • Councilors, who shall include the presidents of designated Korean student and scholar associations at local universities and institutions.

All Councilors must be members of KSEA Philadelphia in good standing. The term of all Councilors is one year, except for Elected Councilors, whose term is three years.

ARTICLE 13. Functions of the Council

The Council shall deliberate and make decisions on items, including, but not limited to:

  • Recommendation of candidates for President, Vice President(s), Auditors, and Elected Councilors.
  • Confirmation of Executive Director and Finance Director.
  • Recommendation to the General Assembly to enact or amend Bylaws.
  • Approval of the Annual Report, business plan, and budget.
  • Establishment of committees.

ARTICLE 14. Election of Elected Councilors

The Elected Councilors shall be elected by the General Assembly, from candidates nominated by the Council or by at least 10 regular members.

ARTICLE 15. Council Meeting

Meetings. Regular Council Meetings shall be convened at least three times per fiscal year.

Quorum. A majority of the Councilors shall be necessary to constitute a quorum.

Voting. Every Councilor shall have one (1) vote. Student Councilors may vote only if they are present in person at the meetings. All questions shall be decided by a majority of the votes cast. In case of a tie vote, the Chair shall have the deciding power.

ARTICLE 16. Salary

No stated salary shall be paid to Councilors for their services. However, a Councilor may serve KSEA Philadelphia in another capacity and receive reasonable compensation for those services.

CHAPTER 4: OFFICERS AND AUDITORS

ARTICLE 17. Officers

The officers of KSEA Philadelphia shall consist of a President, Vice President(s), an Executive Director (serving as Secretary), a Finance Director (serving as Treasurer), a General Director, a Membership Director, and other officers as appointed by the President.

ARTICLE 18. Duties

President. The President shall be the chief executive officer of KSEA Philadelphia, coordinate all offices and committees, and serve as Chair at Council and General Assembly meetings.

Vice President. The Vice President shall assist the President. An Executive Vice President, if designated, serves as President when the President is unable to fulfill the duties of the position.

Executive Director and Finance Director. They shall perform their duties under the direction of the President. The Executive Director shall maintain records of all meetings and serve as Secretary. The Finance Director shall be responsible for all financial matters of the chapter, including budgeting, accounting, and reporting, and serve as Treasurer.

ARTICLE 19. Auditors

Two Auditors shall be elected by the General Assembly. They shall audit KSEA Philadelphia's finances and report the results to the Council. An auditor may not simultaneously serve as an officer or Councilor.

CHAPTER 5: COMMITTEES

ARTICLE 20. Executive Committee

The Executive Committee shall consist of the President, Vice President, Executive Director, Finance Director, and Membership Director. It shall exercise the authority of the Council on matters including business plans, budget, and other items delegated by the Council.

ARTICLE 21. Other Committees

The Council may establish additional committees as needed.

CHAPTER 6: FINANCE

ARTICLE 22. Annual Reports

The final income and expense statement for the previous year and the business plan and budget for the current year shall be reported to all members after approval by the Council.

ARTICLE 23. Income and Expenses

KSEA Philadelphia has the following sources of income:

  • Membership fees.
  • Sponsorships, contributions, and donations from individuals and corporations.
  • Other income derived from KSEA Philadelphia activities.

ARTICLE 24. Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of KSEA Philadelphia shall commence on July 1 and end on June 30 of the following year.

ARTICLE 25. Bank Accounts

The Council shall designate one or more banks as depositories of KSEA Philadelphia funds and shall designate the officers authorized to conduct transactions.

ARTICLE 26. Indemnification

KSEA Philadelphia shall indemnify its Councilors and Officers to the fullest extent permitted by law.

ARTICLE 27. Dissolution

Upon the dissolution of the KSEA Philadelphia Chapter, any assets remaining after payment of or provision for its debts and liabilities shall be distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, or shall be distributed to the federal government, or to a state or local government, for a public purpose. Preferably, such assets will be distributed to the national Korean-American Scientists and Engineers Association (KSEA), provided it is a section 501(c)(3) exempt organization at the time of dissolution.

CHAPTER 7: MISCELLANEOUS

ARTICLE 28. Offices

The KSEA Philadelphia Office shall be located in the Philadelphia area. The principal office may be changed from time to time upon approval of the Council.

ARTICLE 29. Amendment of Bylaws

Amendments to the Bylaws may be recommended by the Council and shall be submitted to the members at an annual or special meeting. Amendments shall require approval of at least two-thirds (2/3) of the total votes cast by a quorum of voting members.

ARTICLE 30. Effective Date

These Bylaws shall be effective immediately upon the approval of the General Assembly.
